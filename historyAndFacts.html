HISTORY AND FUN FACTS

Brief History of Domesticated Cats

The earliest indication of interactions between humans and cats at around 3100 BC in Ancient Egypt. People valued the protection cats provided from killing rodents. Due to Egypt becoming a province of the Roman Empire, many cats had migrated and populated in Europe. From Europe, reportedly cats migrated via the Mayflower to the United States and made their ways into their lives as household pets to many people.

Fact #1.

Cats spend approximately 13-16 hours a day sleeping, making them known to be sleeping for about 70% of their lives.

Fact #2

There are approximately 500 million domesticated cat all around the world as of 2022.

Fact #3

On average, cats live up to about 12 to 15 years of age.

Fact #4

The oldest cat is reportedly 27 years old.
Flossie is a tortoiseshell cat that lives in the UK and got rewarded by the Guinness Book of World record in 2022.

Fact #5

The tallest cat to have ever existed happened to be 18.83 inches.
The cat's name is Fenrir and is an F2 Savannah cat that lives in Detroit, Michigan.

Fact #6

The smallest cat to exist was 5.25 inches.
The tortoiseshell cat named Lilieput, resided in Napa, California and declared the shortest in 2013 by the Guinness book of world records.

How is civilization with cats now?

Considering cats the many cats there are in the world, a survey conducted by today.com states that 41% of American households prefer a cat as a cat, while 77% prefer a dog. Many factors that contribute include the stigma of aggression, independence and, in some cases, the supersitions revolving around cats. This website is aimed to change that stigma and provide some information of what you can expect while having these animals around.
