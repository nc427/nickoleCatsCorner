Brief History of Domesticated Cats

To say the least, cats can portray the most unpredictable set of behaviors. They are the most affectionate creature in the world to biting and scratching your arm in the span of a second.

Some typical behaviors include:

  • Purring: The universal sign of affection and happiness that a cat may display.
  • Grooming: Cat's tend to want to be clean and will do so by licking themselves.
  • Kneading: They turn into little bakers by stretching and retracting their paws in an alternating motion. Just make sure they're doing it on a soft surface like a pillow.
  • Climbing: They climb to the highest height that so they can proclaim themselves as the head of the household.
  • Meowing: Nothing wrong with having a conversation with your little one, but they're most likely very hungry.

Which cat is right for you?

Some factors that can help with narrow down your decisions can be:

  • Family: If you have kids at home, especially small children, try looking for an older cat due to the amount of time a kitten it will take to raise it.
  • Other pets: It takes a while for cats to develop a relationship to another pet in your home.
  • Living arrangements: Some cats do well in small spaces and some like to roam around and explore.

There is a plot twist when "choosing" a cat for you. Basically... the cat will choose you.
You will know deep down when you have made the right decision.
